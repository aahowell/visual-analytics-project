About the Project

The NC Council for Women (CFW) produces the only county-level data on sexual assault and domestic violence services in North Carolina. Domestic violence programs and sexual assault programs are funded by the council and the services that are offered include shelter services, counseling, hsopital, 24 hour hotline servies, transportation, court and advocacy services.This project aims to build a visual interface to present and contextualize NC domestic violence and the agency statistics. A quick visualization of the various services rendered under both the domestic violence ans sexual assault program will allow users to assess how the state fares with respect to these two incidents throughout all the 100 counties involved.

The visualization created with the help of d3 and simple statistics is intended to give an over all view of the state as well as comaparative study across counties. The interactive graphs will allow state officials, service providers and the CFW to better understand annual data and identify trends in service use. A visual analysis of this data will aid officials in identifying survivors’ needs and location; therefore allowing them to focus resources more effectively and provide enhanced help for survivors of interpersonal and sexual violence. Ideally, visualization of this data will allow new ways for CFW and the state to act upon the data available to them.
